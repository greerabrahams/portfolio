Mweb, 2022 to 2024

Brand Manager, Social Media.
Building the foundation.

As Brand Manager for Social Media at Mweb, I led a team of two social media managers and an external team of customer service agents across sales, customer service and technical support. I owned the full social media landscape, from community management through to content briefing and creation, working with both internal teams and external agencies.

As part of the senior marketing team, reporting directly to the Head of Marketing, I collaborated across disciplines on integrated marketing, advertising and PR campaigns. My specific responsibility was to strategise and deploy the social media component of every campaign.

Community Management

Two levels, one consistent voice

My social media managers handled brand community management directly, while also liaising with customer support escalation teams when issues required inter-departmental resolution. Constant cross-functional collaboration, keeping the brand voice consistent while ensuring customer issues moved through the right channels quickly and efficiently.

Content and Campaigns

Strategy through to deployment

Responsible for briefing and overseeing content creation with internal and external agencies, and for strategising and deploying social media across integrated marketing campaigns. Social was not treated as an afterthought. It was built into the campaign architecture from the start.

Sentiment and Reporting

Social as business intelligence

Tracked and measured brand sentiment and client satisfaction on an ongoing basis, translating findings into structured reports shared across the business, including operations, sales, marketing and the customer experience team. Social media fed directly into broader business decision making.

Senior Marketing Team

Cross-functional collaboration

Worked alongside other brand managers as part of the senior marketing team on integrated campaigns, PR and advertising. Brought the social and community perspective into broader marketing strategy, ensuring consistency across every channel and customer touchpoint.

Key Achievement

Led the migration of the entire team from Hootsuite to Qualtrics Social Connect. This was not just a tool switch. I configured all system triggers and workflows, established the brand voice framework and response scripts, and trained all social media agents from scratch. Performance was measured against strict deliverables from day one, with customer happiness as the primary metric.

The Merger, 2024

Then the merger happened.
And everything got bigger.

When Mweb and Webafrica merged, I was promoted to Group Online Reputation Manager. Think Unilever managing Pantene and Organics in the same category. Two distinct audiences, two brand voices, two loyalties, and the constant challenge of serving both without cannibalising either.

I managed one direct report and two indirect team leaders, each heading a dedicated brand team with five agents reporting to them. At its peak this was one of the largest teams in the business overall. The role shifted from implementation to strategic, with KPI management, performance management and structured team development all part of the day to day.

Mweb Team

Team Leader plus 5 agents

Owned the Mweb brand voice, community and reputation across all social channels. Maintained the standards and systems already in place while adapting to the new group structure.

Webafrica Team

Team Leader plus 5 agents

Owned the Webafrica brand voice, community and reputation across all social channels. A distinct identity, a different audience, running in parallel with Mweb without overlap or confusion.

Group Online Reputation Manager, 2024 to 2025

Two brands. One strategy.

The complexity of managing two competing brands in the same category required a strategy that kept both audiences feeling fully served without ever feeling like they were sharing a team. Each brand had its own voice, its own tone, its own community expectations.

My role became increasingly strategic. I contributed to content creation based on insights gathered by my teams, fed intelligence to operations, sales, marketing and customer experience, and ensured both brands maintained the standards and response times their audiences expected.

Key Achievement

Led a second full system migration, this time from Qualtrics Social Connect to Sprout Social across both brands. Rebuilt all automation, triggers and workflows from scratch for two distinct brand identities, recreated the brand voice frameworks and scripts for both teams, and trained the full combined team. Two brands, one platform, no loss of identity on either side.

What This Demonstrates

From manager to strategist.
In three years.

This was not a linear role. It was three years of increasing complexity, a merger, a promotion, two full system migrations, and the challenge of managing two competing brands simultaneously. The growth from implementing social media strategy to owning it at a group level, with a team of twelve, is the headline.

The systems thinking, the people management, the cross-functional collaboration and the ability to keep two distinct brand voices consistent under one roof are the skills that came out of this period.
