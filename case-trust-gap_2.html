The Background

A passion project that became
a real conversation.

The Trust Gap is a Level B-BBEE certified independent digital consultancy based in Cape Town. A firm that helps businesses grow through data-led digital strategy, with a hands-on approach and no agency overhead.

The company had a starter website in place — the structure and content were there, but the look and feel needed work. As someone on the team, I asked if I could take a stab at a fresh concept as a passion project. The result is what you see here.

Using AI as my production tool, I worked from the existing content and restructured the visual presentation, created a new look and feel, and proposed a logo direction. It is currently being considered as the basis for a full rebrand.

My Contribution

The content and messaging architecture belonged to the existing site. My contribution was the visual concept, the layout, the design direction, and the logo. I did not write the copy — I made it look like it deserved to be read.

Hero Headline

Your data already knows
the answer. We help you act on it.

The headline does two things at once: it tells the client their problem is already solvable, and it positions The Trust Gap as the system that turns insight into action. No jargon. No promise of magic. Just a clear statement of what is possible.

Messaging Architecture

Four problems. Named precisely.

The homepage identifies the four failure modes that The Trust Gap's ideal clients recognise in themselves. Each one is written to produce a moment of recognition, not explanation.

01

Data without direction

Dashboards full of numbers, but no model connecting them to the next decision. You know something is there. You just cannot see it yet.

02

Teams talking past each other

Technical and marketing teams work in parallel and rarely in sync. What engineers build and what growth needs never quite lines up.

03

Strategy built for someone else's market

Global playbooks ignore South Africa's complexity. Mobile-only users. Prepaid data. A fundamentally bifurcated economy.

04

No repeatable process

When something works, nobody can say why. When it does not, there is no framework to fix it. Every campaign starts from scratch.

Selected Copy

The words that do the work.

Every line was written to close a specific gap: between what the client knows something is wrong and what they do about it.

Section header — Problem framing

Growth strategies that feel like guesswork are.

The sentence breaks in the middle on purpose. The reader finishes it themselves before the page does. It lands harder that way.

Section header — Solution framing

A system, not a campaign.

Six words that reframe the entire client relationship. Not a retainer. Not a deliverable. A system that belongs to them after we leave.

Market positioning — South Africa specific

South Africa is not one market. We know both.

Acknowledges the mass market and affluent market divide without describing it yet. Creates curiosity and signals local expertise simultaneously.

Core philosophy line

Sustainable growth is not a series of guesses. It is a repeatable science.

The positioning stake in the ground. Everything else on the page points to this.

CTA — Discovery call

No pitch. No pressure. An honest conversation about what is possible.

Removes the fear of being sold to. Positions the call as value regardless of whether they buy. That is how trust is built before a relationship starts.

The Logo

A mark that earns its place.

The logo concept started with the name. The Trust Gap. A gap is not a problem, it is a space between where something is and where it could be. The question was: what visual language captures that idea without being literal or obvious?

The answer came from thinking about what the business actually does. The Trust Gap works with data. It identifies the distance between what a business knows and what it acts on. That is not an abstract concept, it is a measurable one. And there is one universal visual that communicates measurable movement better than anything else: a stock market performance line.

The line moves, dips, recovers, and then hits an inflection point, that single dot where direction changes. That dot is the whole story. It is the moment data becomes a decision. It is the moment the gap starts closing. It is exactly what The Trust Gap sells.

Pairing that mark with a serif wordmark, THE in spaced capitals above, Trust in large italic, GAP in spaced capitals below, creates a lockup that feels both analytical and authoritative. It does not look like a tech startup. It does not look like a traditional consultancy. It looks like something new, which is exactly what The Trust Gap is.

The dot on the line is not decoration. It is the moment everything changes. That is what we do.
