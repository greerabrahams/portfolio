The Real Story

Thrown in at the deep end. Swam anyway.

This is not a polished case study about a smooth client handover. It's the opposite, and that's exactly why it matters.

I came into this role with limited handover. No brand guide. No style documentation. No template library. Just a Canva account with some scattered images and a brief that expected strategy, creation, direction, planning, and implementation, simultaneously, immediately.

Most people would wait for a proper brief. I built one.

1

No brand guide existed. So I created one.

Using Claude AI as an analytical tool, I fed in the existing Canva templates, scattered, inconsistent, undocumented, and extracted a complete brand system from scratch. Colour hex codes, typography hierarchy, spacing rules, layout logic, template type taxonomy. Built from thin air, on the same morning the work was due.

2

Then I built the template library from the guide I'd just written.

14 approved template variants across 5 background types, gradient, blue panel, white, grey dot grid, diagonal line, in both 1080×1080px and 9×16 formats. Every template built to spec. None of these existed this morning. Every single one was produced today, using AI-directed Python generation, working from the brand guide I had extracted minutes before.

3

Then I wrote the strategy, copy, and produced the campaign assets.

With a working brand system now in hand, I briefed and directed the MPhil and PGDip campaigns, graphics, motion video, and platform-specific captions for Instagram, Facebook, and LinkedIn, all on the same day. The approved MPhil graphic and the 21-second PGDip motion graphic below are the outputs.

✓

All of it: one person, one morning, one AI tool, zero prior documentation.

This is what AI-fluent marketing looks like in practice. Not replacement. Acceleration. The strategic judgment, brand instinct, and creative direction are human. The production speed is not.

About the Client

A world-class business school with a distinctly African perspective

The UCT Graduate School of Business is one of Africa's leading business schools, located in Cape Town. Its programmes attract students and faculty from across the continent and the world.

The brief required navigating a very specific tonal balance, and getting it right mattered. UCT GSB's brand speaks with authority earned over decades. Every word had to earn its place.

The Tonal Brief

Proud but not boastful. Warm but not casual. Confident and evidence-led. Africa is always framed as a competitive advantage, never a limitation.

Tools & Process

How this work was made, and why that matters

Every deliverable on this page was produced using AI-directed content production, a modern working method where strategic creative direction is the core skill, and AI tools handle execution at speed.

This is not "AI did it." This is a professional creative director briefing a system with precision, the same instincts that took 15 years to develop, now operating at a pace and scale that wasn't previously possible for a single practitioner.

AI-Directed Workflow

1. Brand brief ingestion

The UCT GSB brand template (14 template variants across 5 background types, 3 orientations) was read and interpreted. Brand rules, dot patterns, logo placement, gradient footer, vertical rule, were extracted and applied.

2. Tone brief translation

The tonal brief (proud not boastful, warm not casual, Africa as advantage) was operationalised into copy constraints and applied consistently across 6 platform captions and 2 graphic headlines.

3. Python-based asset generation

Graphics were produced at exact spec (1080×1080px) using Python image generation, no Canva, no Photoshop. The motion graphic was assembled with Python video tooling: 5 scenes, crossfades, timed to 21 seconds.

4. Human creative direction throughout

Every output was reviewed, refined, and approved against the brief. The AI produced. The creative director decided. That distinction is the skill, and it's not automatable.

Brand Brief & Template Reference

Working from the source, not guessing

The UCT GSB brand template set (14 variants across gradient, blue panel, white, grey dot, and diagonal line backgrounds) was used as the direct brief for all graphic production. Every design decision, colour, logo placement, dot pattern density, footer gradient, maps back to a specific template rule.

This is what separates brand-compliant AI output from generic AI output: the brief quality determines the output quality. Garbage in, garbage out, and the reverse is equally true.
