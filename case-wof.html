About the Organisation

More than firefighting.
Skills, community and real impact.

Working on Fire is an Expanded Public Works Programme operating under the Department of Environment, Forestry and Fisheries, South African National Government, delivered through a public-private partnership model. Beyond firefighting, the programme focuses on fire awareness training, responsible fire use and community upliftment, creating meaningful skills development opportunities for young men and women across the country.

The Work

Real emergencies.
Real consequences.

As National Social Media Specialist, I reported directly to the Head of Communication and worked within the executive communication structure. The role covered the full communication spectrum, from breaking fire alerts to internal stakeholder engagement and public fire safety awareness.

Real-Time Fire Reporting

Breaking fire alerts

The 2021 Cape Town Fire on De Waal Drive. Freestate fires. Real emergencies, fast-paced and demanding. Actively monitored and reported breaking fires across South Africa during both winter and summer fire seasons, ensuring accurate, timely information reached the right audiences, from operational teams to the general public.

Content Creation

On the ground, phone in hand

Created original content including short-form video clips and raw photography captured in the field, feeding material to videographers and other communications specialists as part of the national editorial communications team. Real-world documentation of fire response operations turned into social media content that built public awareness and organisational credibility.

Internal Communication

Stakeholder engagement and collateral

Worked with internal stakeholders to develop fire safety awareness material and communication collateral across social media. Developed the internal social media responsible use policy and trained all firefighters and formal staff across the business. Built the social media strategy to include real-time reporting, pre-made content and editorial content, ensuring consistent messaging across a complex national structure.

Partner Liaison

Coordinating across firefighting associations

Maintained active relationships with firefighting associations across all eight provinces. Kept all parties informed from a social media and communication perspective, particularly during high-pressure fire season periods when speed and accuracy were critical.

Above and Beyond

Incident Command Centre.
COVID-19.

During the COVID-19 pandemic, I was selected as part of the national Incident Command Centre communication team, responsible for reaching, informing and educating over 500 firefighters and staff deployed across South Africa on vaccination protocols, safety procedures, and all COVID compliance requirements.

Firefighters on active deployment could not simply attend a briefing. Communication had to reach them wherever they were, be clear enough to act on immediately, and accurate enough to keep them safe.

International Deployment

100+ South African firefighters dispatched to Alberta, Canada.

The international deployment of over 100 firefighters to assist with wildfires in Alberta, Canada happened during COVID, which added significant logistical and communication complexity to an already demanding operation. Supporting the messaging and public communication around a deployment of that scale, under those conditions, remains one of the standout professional moments of my career.
