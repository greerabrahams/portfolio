Virgin Active: From disruption to dialogue

Virgin Active South Africa was refurbishing gyms into health and wellness centres. Someone had to make sure the members knew about it, were ready for it, and didn't cancel because of it. That was me.

The Situation

Virgin Active South Africa was refurbishing clubs across the network, turning gyms into full health and wellness destinations. For members, that meant construction, closed facilities, disrupted routines, and access changes, often with very little notice.

For paying members this wasn't a minor inconvenience. It was a direct hit on a service they'd committed to. The risk was real: frustration, cancellations, reputational damage. What the programme needed was one person at the intersection of operations, project management, and member communication. Someone who made sure nothing fell through the gap.

The refurbishment changed the gym. The communication determined whether members stuck around for it.

Types of Communication

The work spanned four distinct types of communication, each with its own audience, urgency, and tone requirements. Change management wasn't a separate workstream. It ran through all four.

01

Project Communications

Translated Scope of Works and construction plans into member-facing communication plans. Coordinated with project managers, coordinators, and club management to keep all stakeholders aligned from refurbishment start to reopening.

02

Emergency Communications

Unplanned closures, last-minute facility changes, urgent member notices. Written fast, sent faster. If a facility closes at 6am, the member at the door at 5:45 needed to know yesterday.

03

Operational Communications

The consistent, ongoing layer of member communication: new joiner welcomes, billing reminders, payment notices, membership provision updates. Routine by nature, but critical to retention.

04

Campaign Communications

Full communication plans for member-facing campaigns: new yoga classes, exclusive Premier events, special class launches. Copy, scripts, timelines, and cross-functional coordination from brief to send.

The Channels

Five channels. Each with its own format constraints, audience expectations, and copy requirements. The same message, written five different ways, timed to work together, not compete.

Three Audiences. One Club Network.

No club member is the same, and the communication reflected that. Three distinct audiences, each with different expectations, preferences, and relationship with the brand. The standard of service was the same across all three. What changed was the tone, the framing, and how we spoke to each group.

Club Type Member Profile Communication Approach
Club Broad membership base; value-conscious; routine-driven Clear, practical, action-oriented. Strong emphasis on alternative access arrangements and minimising impact on daily routine.
Premier Health-conscious; experience-led; higher service expectations Elevated tone. Refurbishment framed as an investment in their experience, not just a disruption to endure.
Collection Premium clubs; long-standing, loyalty-sensitive members Highest level of care applied at every touchpoint. Every friction point anticipated and addressed before it reached the member.

Audience Segmentation & BI Collaboration

A well-written message sent to the wrong list is a worse outcome than no message at all. Before any communication went out, the audience had to be right.

I worked closely with the Business Intelligence team to ensure member lists were correctly pulled, structured, and verified in Excel before entering the send pipeline. Lists were segmented by club, tier, membership status, and geography, signed off before anything moved.

This is where technical rigour and communication precision meet. It's unglamorous work. It's also where the most costly errors happen, and where getting it right makes everything else downstream credible.

Why this matters

Segmentation is not an afterthought

Member data across multiple club types and membership categories, each with different communication needs, different entitlements, and different sensitivities. Getting the list wrong means the wrong member gets a closure notice for a club they don't belong to, or a payment reminder with the wrong billing date. The BI collaboration was not support work. It was part of the communication function.
